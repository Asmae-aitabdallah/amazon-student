Supporting a young person considering T-Levels

How T-Levels compare

  • vs A-Levels: A-Levels are academic and usually taken as three separate subjects. One T-Level is a single, larger qualification focused on a career area, with a built-in work placement.
  • vs apprenticeships: An apprenticeship is mostly on-the-job (around 80%) and paid. A T-Level is mostly classroom-based (around 80%) with a shorter placement, better suited to someone not yet ready to commit to one job.

What progression looks like

  • T-Levels carry UCAS points, so university is a realistic route. In the 2024 cycle, the majority of T-Level applicants to higher education secured a place.
  • They also lead into higher and degree apprenticeships or directly into skilled work.
  • Students must also work towards GCSE maths and English if not already achieved at grade 4.

Questions worth asking the college

  • Which T-Level subjects do they offer, and what are the entry requirements?
  • How is the industry placement organised and supported?
  • What progression have past students gone on to?

This is general guidance. Provision differs between colleges, so confirm specifics directly with the provider and at gov.uk.
