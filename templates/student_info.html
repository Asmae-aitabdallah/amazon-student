T-Levels: what they are and what to expect

The basics

  • T-Levels are two-year, Level 3 technical qualifications for 16-19 year olds in England, taken after GCSEs.
  • One T-Level is broadly equivalent in size to three A-Levels and involves around 1,800 hours of study over the two years.
  • There are 21 T-Levels currently available across areas like digital, healthcare, engineering, construction, business and education.
  • They are graded Pass, Merit, Distinction, and Distinction*.

The industry placement

  • Every T-Level includes a placement with an employer of at least 315 hours, roughly 45 days.
  • The placement is about 20% of your time; the rest is classroom learning.
  • It can be done as one block or split across days, depending on the course and employer.

Where they can take you

  • University: T-Levels carry UCAS tariff points (a Distinction* is worth 168, the same as A*A*A*).
  • Higher or degree apprenticeships.
  • Skilled employment in your chosen sector.

Entry requirements and available subjects vary by provider. Check the official list at gov.uk and your local college before applying.
