For educators

Delivering and advising on T-Levels

Structure of a T-Level

  • Core component (around 400-600 guided learning hours): underpinning knowledge, assessed by external written exam.
  • Occupational specialism (600+ hours): technical skills and behaviours for a specific occupation.
  • Industry placement: minimum 315 hours with an employer.

Employer engagement

  • Placements are central, so building a reliable employer network is the main delivery challenge.
  • If one employer cannot host the full 315 hours, a placement can be shared between two organisations with aligned learning goals.
  • Higher education providers can also host placements, which can support local progression partnerships.

Support available

  • Professional development for T-Level delivery is available from the Education and Training Foundation at no charge to providers.
  • Awarding organisations publish detailed specifications per T-Level to support curriculum mapping.
  • UCAS provides tariff information, including partial-achievement allocations, to advise learners on HE routes.

Figures and arrangements change; verify against gov.uk and the relevant awarding organisation for current guidance.
